    About the School

The Oxford School on Neutron Scattering, established in 1989, is intended primarily for scientists who are new to the field of neutron scattering. Lectures and tutorials covering all aspects of the theory and practice of neutron diffraction and spectroscopy will be given by international experts. Techniques and applications at both steady state and pulsed neutron sources are covered, and the school gives the  opportunity to hear about some of the exciting new research being carried out in neutron scattering. Participants in the School are also encouraged to give a short overview of their own research during the tutorial sessions.

    School fees and allocation of places

The cost per student for the Oxford School on Neutron Scattering is £ 325 (€ 486)

There are 35 places available for students attending the school. Those who have pre-registered will be offered places preferentially in the event that the School is oversubscribed.

The closing date for registrations is 00:00 BST 6 July 2005 and applicants will be notified if they have been accepted for the School by 5 August 2005.

We are grateful to the CCLRC and NMI3 network for their generous financial support. This has allowed us to offer substantial reductions in school fees to all students. Unfortunately, this means that we are not able to offer individual students further support either for travel fees or attendance bursaries.

Students may be able to obtain contributions towards the cost of the summer school from university or company travel and training funds or from subject interest groups of professional societies such as the Institute of Physics or the Royal Society of Chemistry. Applications should be made direct to these organisations.

All school fees must be paid in advance and before 15 August 2005. There will be no facilities for fee payment available during the school.

    Programme and course materials

The programme consists of lectures, tutorials and problem classes taking place each week day from 0900 to 1700 with occasional evening lectures. In addition, students are invited to make short presentations to the school about their current research projects.

The teaching language of the school will be English.

All students will receive a copy of the classic neutron scattering text book by Gordon Squires "Introduction to the theory of thermal neutron scattering" in addition to copies of notes from the School lecturers.

There will also be an opportunity to visit the nearby ISIS Pulsed Neutron and Muon Source.
